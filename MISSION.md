# Mission: Mechanistic Interpretability for Alignment

## Why

I worry about the risk that ASI will arrive before research advances sufficiently to align it. There
is a huge incentive for building capabilities and capacity, but alignment is comparatively
neglected.

I choose **interpretability specifically**, applying neglectedness reasoning to myself. I'm
currently more qualified to contribute in other areas (e.g. systems design, evals) — but those labor
pools are already large, so my marginal contribution there is small. Interp's labor pool is thin and
its barrier to entry is high, contributing toward its neglectedness. I can clear that barrier, and
I'm drawn to it on a technical level.

## Success looks like

- I can read current interpretability papers fluently and explain them from first principles.
- I can reproduce canonical results from scratch at a smaller scale.
- I can make contributions that future peers would recognize as real.
- I am ready to hold an IC role where advancing interpretability is my day job.

## Factors

- **Time.** My day job is management; technical depth gets built in the margins. I need a fast pace
  due to time constraints and maintain accurate understanding of my own comprehension level.
- **Financial stability.** I can afford to optimize for qualification over certification. No need to
  prioritize signal over substance.
- **Foundation.** I have ~14y industry (Amazon, Google), strong systems/engineering, and a long AI
  throughline (undergrad intelligence spec, ML analysis, AI-dev tooling) to build upon.

## Out of scope

- **Lateral role moves.** If speed-to-role were the goal, I would go through more standard interview
  preparation. If I were to pursue that, I would do so separately from this effort.
- **Systems design / governance / evals.** I'm already more qualified there and the pool of
  qualified labor is healthy.
- **Implementation below the linear-algebra layer** (autodiff internals, BLAS). Deferred — torch
  tensor ops are the bottom primitive (see the Automatic Differentiation side quest).
